**Key Measures For Prosperity**

The "Singaporean economic miracle" was driven by a series of strategic reforms and policies implemented since independence in 1965. These reforms transformed Singapore from a resource-poor port city into a global economic powerhouse. Key reforms include:

### **Pro-Business Environment**

- **Tax Incentives**: Low corporate taxes, tax holidays (Companies were promised 5-10 years of no taxes), and exemptions for foreign investors to attract multinational corporations (MNCs).
- **Ease of Doing Business**: Streamlined regulations and business practices.
- **Economic Development Board (EDB)**: Established in 1961 to drive industrialization, attract FDI, and develop key sectors like electronics and petrochemicals.

### **Investment in Human Capital**

- **Education Reforms**: Focus on bilingualism (English + local tongue), technical/vocational training (e.g., Institute of Technical Education), and STEM education to build a skilled workforce.
- **Global Talent Recruitment**: Policies to attract foreign professionals while upskilling locals.

### **Infrastructure Development**

- **World-Class Port and Airport**: Development of Jurong Port and Changi Airport as global logistics hubs.
- **Urban Planning**: Efficient public transport, housing (via the Housing Development Board, HDB) which created public housing, and industrial estates (e.g., Jurong Industrial Estate).

### **Political Stability and Governance**

- **Anti-Corruption Measures**: Strict enforcement via the Corrupt Practices Investigation Bureau (CPIB).

### **Trade Openness**

- **Free Trade Agreements (FTAs)**: Over 20 FTAs, including with the U.S., China, and EU.
- **Port Liberalization**: Minimal trade barriers and tariffs, positioning Singapore as a global trading hub.

### **Financial Sector Development**

- **Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)**: Ensured stable currency and prudent financial regulation.
- **Wealth Management Hub**: Tax incentives and secrecy laws attracted private banking and fintech firms.

### **Social Policies**

- **Central Provident Fund (CPF)**: Mandatory savings scheme for housing, healthcare, and retirement, reducing state welfare burdens.
- **Public Housing (HDB)**: Affordable housing for 80% of the population, fostering social stability.

### **Economic Diversification**

- Shifted from labor-intensive manufacturing to high-value sectors like biotech, finance, and digital services.
- Focus on innovation via agencies like A*STAR and R&D investments.

### **Labor Market Strategies**

- **Tripartite Model**: Collaboration between government, employers, and unions to minimize strikes and ensure wage flexibility.
- **Foreign Workforce Policies**: Supplemented local labor with controlled inflows of foreign workers.

### **Sustainability and Adaptation**

- **Green Economy**: Investments in renewable energy, water recycling (NEWater), and carbon reduction.
- **Digitalization**: Early adoption of tech (e.g., e-payments, AI) to maintain competitiveness.

### Outcome:

These reforms created a resilient, globally integrated economy with a GDP per capita rising from $500 (1965) to over $72,000 (2022). Singapore’s success stemmed from pragmatic governance, adaptability, and a relentless focus on efficiency and innovation.
