# Mastering Selling

Selling is a skill that is severely undervalued yet it is one of the most important skills to make money, to secure relationships and thrive. Providing something that people want, or desire, or like, or need or would find interesting and monetizing this exchange is essential for a thriving enterprise, be it professional or personal.

So I John Victor, intend to teach exactly how to sell and close deals. Understand that selling is about supplying your client with what is valuable to them and monetizing the exchange.

**The Art Of Selling**

Selling is a particularly broad ability, selling is convincing someone to exchange their valuable offerings to your valuable offerings, it is exchanging goods and services for money but it also is exchanging contact numbers, setting a meeting, commiting to a relationship, setting a business partnership, engaging in anything with you, the same principle applies: Convince someone for a Qui pro quo, Tik for Tak, Win Win, you offer something nice they offer something nice. The means are important: It can be on the phone, on a website, on an adverting, in person. Usually the biggest investment in engagement someone makes the easier it is to close a deal. If you can, always try to close in person or on the phone as it is more personal and more engaging, it is more real than an advertising on the internet and has better closing rates, yet the scalability of the internet and other means is unmatched, analyse the means.

**How to Sell**

Anytime you need to sell something: Plan and architecture, make a sale script if you have to, rehearse the proposal and the approach, architecture the sales like a professional, try to assess what would persuade your client and rehearse on that.

Any sale is to be made with these steps:

***Fostering Affinity:*** You need to establish a deep sense of affinity with the person you're selling to, signal you know your craft and you want to help, signal you have something in common with the person, make the person feel comfortable and genuinely perceiving you as someone that knows your craft, have something in common a common clique or affiliation, foster affinity yet make the person perceive as someone that can help.

***Understand The Wants and Needs:*** Now you need to understand the person, what this client needs very much, what this client wants, what would be exciting for this client, what moves this client, what is attractive to this client, their wants. Talk to this client and get to know that deeply, specially in how it relates to anything you are offering. Convey the sense that you are trying to help this client.

***Package Your Offering***: Now knowing everything about the person you need to package whatever you are offering to fit to what this person wants and desires. Make it primal, tap into something the person wants to avoid, bring a solution, tap into desire, tap into needs. Make your solution seem like everything the person actually needs in the moment.

***Solve Objections***: When you make and package your offering you might initially, in talking to the person or when making your offering you might pick up on objections, these are paradigms that the person makes up or are preconceived that go in opposite direction to closing the deal. Solve these objections, make the person understand your offering better, make it clear that it brings value to to them, whatever the objections someone might make up, address them, with a twist, even indirectly. Affirm that the product solves their problem, affirm that it is good, affirms that it brings value. Solve the objections.

***Close The Deal***: Close the deal with the person, user affirmative and confident language, trigger action to make the person actually close the deal. Be natural and affirmative, be nice, make it clear it's a win win situation and close the deal.

***Try Again*:** If you tried to close the deal and the sale didn't come through, fret not, keep the contact, keep in touch and try again to actually sell the product again in a similar manner, maybe incorporation novel information a different offering. And they to close again. Sometimes it's the client that haven't received the information well, sometimes it's the offering that didn't compell, try again.

**Maxims In Selling You Should Know**

- Always present yourself as a professional, in your field or as someone that knows what they are doing, how you are presenting yourself is very important and makes all the difference.
- Charity only works with good people and they are not common. Always appeal to self interest, or needs, desires, aspirations, and wants. Tap into what makes people move, what makes them tick.
- Make it always seems like a win win situation, don't make it seem like you're a sales’ person, but make it seem like you are presenting the value that they desire.
- Match your client energy and build each other energy into taking closing action. This makes them feel comfortable with you, engage with them to lift their energy by making them excited to act, but don't mismatch their energy if they don't reciprocate the arousal properly.
- Pay attention how you say things, use your best tone. Confident and sure.
- Rehearse to perfection.
- Ignite passion, ignite passion to you selling, tap into desire, wants, narrative and paradigms. Ignite their passion in your selling proposal.
