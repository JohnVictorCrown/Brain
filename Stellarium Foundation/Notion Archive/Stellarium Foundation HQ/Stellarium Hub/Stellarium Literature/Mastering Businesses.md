**The Art Of Raising Money**

The first thing you should know about business is that you need to ask for money always, you need to ask for money for the bank, you need to ask for money from investors and institutions. To raise money just ask for it, if you have a business draft or a business plan, recruit a board of members, CEO, and other collaborators and ask for money from investors, call potential venture capital and pitch your business, call angel investors and pitch and make meetings, call banks and present your business, if you don't know banks actually invest in businesses, just assemble a great team, a great business plan and documents and a solid and confident proposal and you could walk with $100 million from a bank to build the business for exchange in equity in your company. Ask, ask, ask.

If your business is of a philanthropic and altruistic nature you can ask for money and pay with donations and other sources of revenue.

**The Two Main, And That's It** 

The thing to know about business is that it is about money making, *you need to increase revenue and revenue potential and decrease cost*s, these two things and that's it. 

To decrease costs generally it is of paramount importance to have a *scientific and pragmatic approach*, this is the financial approach: decreasing costs by outlining everything that actually produces income in the company and redlining everything that there is related costs and doesn't produce revenue for the company in any way. With the redlined costs, optimize it to the point of low or no expenditure. 

Then take a *systematic approach*: Whatever has costs for the company structure, how can I create systems that serves the same purpose yet are more efficient and expend less money? It can be anything from restructuring of teams, management structure or roles, to creating Artificial Intelligence Systems with superhuman performance and decrease costs and increase productivity and expertise, or optimizing whole structures, such as using heavy automation in a customer support team, etc… Anything that can be tweaked to increase productivity and is more efficient: Do it. You need a lot of creativity, generalist knowledge and heavy advice with consultants.

To Increase Revenue, your work as a manager is paramount and I teach you, you need to leverage the collective creativity that your company can lift, creating systems in that anyone on the company is welcome to suggest improvements in the company easily and freely. Your work as a manager is also of a strategist, be creative, understand the industry and what it could be, get pen and pad and extensively dream about new ideas and ways to monetize your product and service. To increase revenue there are certain established ways to do it: Marketing strategies, consolidating markets with economics of scale, markup strategies, increasing the perceived value of the product, adding features, focusing on better markets such and B2B, fine tunning your product offering, increasing your product offering to more markets, launch novel products and services, expanding your offering in the industry, etc…

Expand what makes you money: Analyse your own business, what and how you do that currently makes you money: if all your revenue come from sales, expand, scale and optimize sales in your company. Outline how you currently make money and scale, expand and optimize to increase revenue. It might seem intuitive but that's an exercise that might be neglected.

Be creative and be bold, this is the most important way, finding ways to increase revenue.

**Managing Personnel and Systems**

Corporate work is very social despite what some would want to believe and contrary to common sense *your job as a manager is to make it less social and more like a system of inputs and outputs*, for this you will need to make use of deep [***Structural Incentive Engineering:*** How Everything Is a System And Why You Need to Engineer It](Structural%20Incentive%20Engineering%20How%20Everything%20Is%20fce076cddc9a407491836ebb90ee4435.md) (Read The Link To Master This Science). 

First step is to optimize the incentive structure to their function: This means that you need to analyse all the interactions and processes on your company or team and engineer it to increase productivity, efficiency and revenue making. For managers this might mean a big part of their income is tied to their performance in equity creation (Assets - Liabilities), or tied to *tangible performance maximizing equations* with variables like revenue creation, productivity, satisfaction, etc… 

Read More on [Why Efficiency Is Necessary: State Companies, Public Spending and Social Security](Why%20Efficiency%20Is%20Necessary%20State%20Companies,%20Publi%201a7c1c04bbc18044a28fdb936f31005d.md)  

Imagine a CEO of an state energy company that has the compensation based on these equation:

Base Salary * ( Revenues / All Costs)* (Shareholders Equity / Liabilities) * 1/Overall cost per kWh

This would incentivize the CEO to increase efficiency and decrease costs in energy production, maximize shareholders equity while decreasing liabilities and work on decreasing the cost for the consumer. Engineer well and pay attention to counter productive ways to maximize metrics without maximizing the performance.

The next step is to make all jobs’ performance quantifiable: For customer support this means a score in customer satisfaction, for marketing this means conversions, for sales this means sales, for managers this means macro metrics such as equity creation or productivity growth, try to quantify every single job and make compensation fairly and generously tied to measurable metrics, this objectifies incentive engineering and fine tune it for growth, this also means that employees only benchmark is their own metrics and not anything else, which is important.

The next step is to systemize every social system for simplicity and efficiency: For dealing with social settings make everything into a simple and effortless protocol with no bureaucracy, every employee needs a systematic way of doing anything in the company easily, effortlessly, fast and efficiently, the less social work and paperwork they need to do, the better the system is.

The next step is to: Delegate, delegate, delegate. There is so much you can do as a manager, your job as manager is to leverage your team, start delegating tasks, don't let projects and plans be in the drawer, delegate research and work to personnel that can do it, your job is that of a scientist, a master, and an orcherstrator. Leverage your potential and your team’s potentials: Delegate delegate, delegate.

The next step is to implement Kaizen (Continuous Improvement): As a manager you need to create streamlined systems for all people in your team to return written feedback, make it as easily as writing a comment in a chat even anonymously, feedback on suggestions of systematic improvements, opportunities of products to be launched, and markets to unravel, for everything your team or company members need to be your best advisors, they are working under you, with fine tuned incentives for performance so they will collectively seek to increase the whole team or company performance, this is the magic of well engineered incentive systems. As the wise said: “In the multitude of counselors there is safety, in the multitude of counselors purposes are established.” Create and encourage their suggestion in anything in the working systems of the company, any improvement in the way of doing things, or technological potentials for increased performance. Face these suggestions as valuable opportunities that a consulting company might charge hundreds of thousands of dollars and might not even be as effective as the suggestions of your well incentivized, creative, and well motivated personnel. Encourage and reward good suggestions and feedback on anything. You can even take this a step further and make your whole team or company under you, your decentralized advisors by presenting problems, opportunities and research and invite their suggestions and solutions.

**Principles and Maxims In Business**

- To sell anything, you need to understand what the person needs or wants and offer this to them as your product or service.
- Make everything as simple, fast, clear and easy as possible, specially social settings. No bureaucracy, no complex systems, no frizzle. Make it clear that the way to do a good job is to up their role’s benchmark.
- Everything is a system, understand how the money system work: Increase Revenue, Decrease Costs. Increase Assets and Decrease Liabilities. Increase Growth, Decrease Waste.
- Delegate, delegate delegate. Never let a good work go to waste, delegate the work, hire expertise if you have to.
- Always ask, always ask anything you want, be it money, favors, experiences, be tactful and always ask what you want. It's how the money system works: You ask money for the bank, for investors, for institutions, favors from people, opportunities, ***always ask***.
- Always have counselors, people that run you through new opportunities, that research the market and ways to increase revenue and optimize, people to suggest improvements in any facet of the business.
- Always be ambitious, never greedy, always bold but not unrealistic.
- Be wise and rational, but be creative and dauntless.
- Make regular check-ins on everything you work on, mediate on the systems in place and *assess actions you can take to improve the systems or processes* regularly, as in everyday or a couple of times a week.
- Always do your due diligence. e.g. If you're gonna enter a new market assess all the implications even in your own judgement, ask people to research, and do your own due diligence. Yet be dauntless and avoid inaction due to over engineering, be fast.
- Develop and trust your intuition.
- You make your people collaborators working under you satisfied with their jobs which is important for job retention, morale, motivation. You need to delegate jobs or functions in a meaningful way, give someone a function or job, metrics and guidelines and give them full autonomy on how they can themselves care take and fulfill this function. It's very important to understand and make functions based on that.
