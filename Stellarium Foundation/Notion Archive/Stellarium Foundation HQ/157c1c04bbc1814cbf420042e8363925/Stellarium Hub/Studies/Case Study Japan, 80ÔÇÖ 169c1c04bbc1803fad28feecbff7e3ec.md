# Case Study: Japan, 80’

- Post war economy, thriving exports
- Plaza Accord's was implemented to increase the value of the yen against US dollar. It affected exports.
- The Plaza accord was made by the G7 countries to make US exports more competitive by devaluing the US dollar, they did this by selling us dollar reserves.
- BOJ decreased interest rates to increase inflation and devalue the yen against the US dollar to make exports more competitive again.
- This caused asset prices but not consumer price increases and the economic strength of the japanese economy attracted further foreign investment, increasing demand for the yen.
- The relationship between interest rates and currency value is more influence by investment demand, decreasing interest rates does not necessarily increase currency value.
- Cheap money, made investors to get many loans, collaterized to land and real state assets, this drove demand for real state, increasing prices.
- Stock market soared due to increased investment and inflated asset prices.
- When the BOJ increased interest rates this made money more expensive and decreased demand for real state and stocks, collapsing collateralized debt.
