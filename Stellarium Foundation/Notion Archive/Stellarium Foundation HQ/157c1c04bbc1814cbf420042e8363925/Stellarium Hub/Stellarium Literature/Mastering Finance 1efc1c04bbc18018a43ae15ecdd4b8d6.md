## Macroeconomics: A Necessary Introduction

You need to understand macroeconomy not only to understand the news, but to understand how to conduct your business effectively and how to make money from financial systems. Here is everything you need to understand and about macroeconomics and how to economical system works.

The economy and made of the exchange of goods and services. Goods are exchanged through trade, every good that comes into the country comes by ship of plane into the customs of the country. Every service is done through companies in the country or abroad or online and though other means. Companies and consumers form the basis of the economy, companies provide goods and services and consumers consume goods and services. The banks are fundamental for of the economy because they manage the liquidity of the country by providing money and credit to business and consumers and for intermediating the exchange of money.

**Employment & Inflation**

Employment is generally measured as how many people in the country are ativately working and making money. This is very important because the more people producing and *making* money the more economic activity and consumer spending, high employment means a better economic system, overall prosperity, and a better spending potential for the government. Inflation is the rate of how much and basic basket of goods are rising in price, low interest rates are good, specially because inflation is a tax on the poor. Inflation is risen due to money in the broad economy or money velocity, that means if the is more money on the hands of consumers being spent the higher the price of goods tend to rise. Money in the bank does not contribute to higher inflation, money in the financial market does not necessarily contribute to higher inflation, this is an important wisdom to know, only money in the broad economy contribute to higher inflation.

**Interest Rates And Central Banks**

The central bank is an independent institution tasked with managing the stability and well functioning of the economy, they do this managing liquidity and interest rates. The tools at their disposal vary much, such as setting the percentage of money banks need to keep with them for their total amount of deposits (reserve requirements), buying and selling bonds and financial assets, giving money to consumers to increase inflation or maintaining economic stability, or doing virtually anything that involves spending money that directly contributes to the stability and well functioning of the economy, they do this by printing money out of thin air yet don't be ignorant as this is a feature of the economy and how it is supposed to work. 

The objective of the central is to almost always keep inflation low and employment high, this is wisdom: The central bank should print as much money as long as it keeps inflation low and employment high, this is a feature of the economic system.

When the central bank increase interest rates, that means the incentive engineering is such that for banks, that create most of the consumer money it is better for them to keep a bigger part of their money in treasuries and risk free assets than to loan this money to companies and consumers, this means that it decreases the amount of loans and money circulating in the broad economy, therefore it decreases inflation. When it decreases interest rates, the return incentive is such that banks need to capitalize their deposits better by offering more loans and therefore increasing the amount of loans with lower interest rates increasing money in the economy, leading to a more pronounce inflation. Lower interest rates also usually mean less unemployment because there is more money on the economy, more investment, more expansion, more growth. **Derivatives: Futures And Options**

They are financial securities that underlie an asset such as commodities, indexes, currencies, and stock. 

The advantage is that you can invest leveraged, that means that you can invest a much smaller amount of money of each trade and get much higher movements. Let's say you want to invest in the S&P500 index, instead of investing in the company stock and buying shares, that to have significant movements in price you might need thousands of dollars, you can buy options or S&P500 index futures and with a few hundred dollars have the same kind of return for a small movement in the market, obviously take care of volatility and risk management. 

Options are securities that entitles you to buy or sell the underlying asset at a maturity, let's say a stock, you buy an option to buy or sell the underlying stock, and then at the maturity date you can exercise the option and buy or sell the underlying stock at the strike price with the option you paid for.

Options are a great way to make money leveraged when you want to make plays. It's leveraged, yet there are intricacies, the price is empirically ruled by the Black-Sholes option pricing equation the Greeks. 

The price of an option varies very much with the change in the price of the asset and at the pace that maturity nears the price of the option becomes more and more volatile with a change in the underlying asset. 

You can use options to hedge and to trade. Let's say you want to use options to hedge so you buy shares of a company and also buy a put option on the money with the same strike price so if the stock value decreases in value, at maturity you can exercise your option and buy the underlying shares with the strike price of the option losing a small amount of money.

There are other ways to trade options, study how it works and if you have a play or trade to make, leverage options.
